Instructions of Use

: Allowed by the classic's Owner

: Allowed by Restoration shops

: Allowed by Certification authorities shops

: Allowed by all users

Login:

How to login to an already registered account?

1º: If not logged in, the Login page will be your homepage. (You can also access it by clicking in the "Login" button on the top right corner, or by clicking in our logo)

2º: Fill in the boxes with your email address and password, and choose your associated organization.

3º: Click the "Submit" button.

Register a new Account:

How to register a new account?

1º: In the Login page, click the "register" link/button. This will redirect you to the Register page.

2º: Fill in all the fields:

  • First Name / Type of Entity
  • Surname / Name of the Company
  • Country
  • Email
  • Password (repeat the password)
  • Organization

3º: Check the box confirming all your details are correct and click the "Register" button.

Check your Classics:

How to check all the cars I own?

1º: If logged in, you can click the "Your Classics" button in the Navigation Bar, which is available on top of each page.

2º: This will redirect you to the page where the classics owned by the logged user are display. Some additional details from each vehicle are also displayed. The button to redirect to each Vehicle Card is also displayed.

Check a Classic Vehicle Card:

How to check a specific Vehicle Card?

1º: In the Classics page, click the "Vehicle Card" button.

2º: When the page is loaded, it is displayed basic information about the vehicle, such as make, model, VIN, prior owners and other in the " Vehicle Data" Tab

3º: On the top right corner, you can click the "Click to Copy Link" button to automatically copy the link of that classic, to then share with another user. (This user must have been granted permissions priorly in order to be able to have access to that vehicle.)

4º: If logged in as the owner of the classic, 4 buttons are displayed: " Edit Information", " History", " Access Permissions" and " Access History". For other users, only 1 button is displayed, which is the " History" button.

5º: To check the Restorations applied you can click the Dropdown named "  Restorations". This will open a new Tab with cards for each restoration applied.

6º: Each Restoration card has information about:

  • Restoration title
  • Restoration description
  • Who made the restoration (it is possible to click it to get more information about the user/entity)
  • The date and time when the restoration was added
  • A button to edit the details and photos (only who originally made it is able to edit it)
  • A button to display the photos associated.

7º: For Owners and Restoration entities a button " Add new Procedure" is displayed.

8º: To check Certifications and Extra documents you can click the the Dropdown named "  Certifications & Other Documentation". This will open a new Tab with information about the entities who certified the vehicle and any other extra documents associted.

9º: For Certification authorities a "Certify" button is displayed.

10º: For Owners, Restoration entities, and Certification authorities a " Add New Document" button is displayed.

Edit your classic:

How can I update the information about a particular vehicle?

1º: To access this page, click the " Edit Information" button on the Classic Card page. Only the owner of the vehicle has access to the Edit page.

2º: Update all the desired fields. From the basic details, only the VIN/Chassis number is not possible to update.

3º: Click the "Update Info" button to update the information.

Check a Classic's History:

How can I check the history of updates of my Vehicle Card?

1º: To access this page, click the " History" button on the Classic Card page. Every user with access to the particular vehicle can access this page.

2º: In this page, you can check the data of all the versions of the classic with the associated Blockchain transaction ID. You can check the basic details and the resto is organized in 4 different Tabs: " Prior Owners", " Restorations", " Certified By" and " Extra Documents"

3º: Every version is organized by date and time when the transaction was performed.

Check and Update a Classic's Access Permissions:

How can I grant or revoke access permissions from a specific car to another user?

1º: To access this page, click the " Access Permissions" button on the Classic Card page. Only the owner of the vehicle has access to the Access Permissions page.

2º: In this page, you can check all the users that have access permissions to a specific vehicle, their access level (viewer, modifier or certifier) and when the access was granted.

3º: It is also possible to access or grant access permissions. For this, you need to provide: if you want to "Grant" or "Revoke" permissions; the access level (viewer, modifier, certifier); and the user to grant/revoke. All this is auto-filled, you only need to select and then need to click the "Change permissions" button.

4º: Ultimately, you can also transfer the ownership of your classic to another user. This will grant the "Owner" access level to the other user and your access is completly removed automatically. To do this, you only need to select the user to transfer the classic to, check the box confirming you want to transfer your vehicle and click the "Update Owner" button.

Check a Classic's Access History:

How can I check all the users that, at some point in time, had access to my classic?

1º: To access this page, click the " Access History" button on the Classic Card page. Only the owner of the vehicle has access to the Access History page.

2º: In this page, you can check the data of all the versions of the access permissions with the associated Blockchain transaction ID. For each version, you can check the owner and the users with viewer, modifier, or certifier permissions, and the date their access was granted.

3º: Every version is organized by date and time when the transaction was performed.

Add a new Restoration Step:

How can I add a new Restoration procedure to a classic?

1º: To access this page, click the " Add new Procedure" button on the Classic Card page. Only the owner and restoration entities have access to the Add new Restoration page.

2º: Fill in the title and description fields of the Restoration. If the desired, you can also add photos to associate with that restoration procedure.

3º: To finish, you need to click the "Create" button.

Update a Restoration Step:

How can I update a Restoration procedure?

1º: !First is important to highlight that only the user who originally created the restoration procedure is allowed to update it!

2º: To access this page, click the button on the desired restoration card on the Classic Card page.

3º: Once on the page, you can update the title and description fields, as well as, add more photos to that restoration.

4º: Click the "Update Info" button to update the restoration data.

Add a new Document:

How can I add a new document to my classic?

1º: To access this page, click the " Add New Document" button on the Classic Card page. Only the owner, restoration entities and certification authorities have access to the Add new Document page.

2º: Once on the page, you need to fill the title of the document and add the document. This document can be a file of any type: image, video, PDF or any other.

3º: Click the " Add document" button to add the document.

Create a classic: [Restoration shops and Certification authorities exclusive]

As a restoration shop or cerification authority, how can I a new classic to the platform?

1º: If logged in as a restoration shop, you can click the "New Classic" green button in the list of classics page.

2º: This will redirect you to the page where you will have to fill some basic details about the classic, such as make, model, country of manufacture VIN/chassis number (which can not be editted later), and owner (from a list of registered users).

3º: Check the box confirming that you want to create the classic and that the VIN/chassis number is correct.

4º: Click the "Create Classic" button to create the new classic.

Get all the classics that have modifier access to: [Restoration shops exclusive]

As a restoration shop, how can I check all the classics that I have modifier access to?

1º: If logged in as a restoration shop, you can click the "Your Classics" button in the Navigation Bar, which is available on top of each page.

2º: This will redirect you to the page where a list of classics that have been granted the permission to be modified by you are displayed. Some additional details from each vehicle are also displayed. The button to redirect to each Vehicle Card is also displayed.

Get all the classics that have certifier access to: [Certification authorities exclusive]

As a certification authority, how can I check all the classics that I have certifier access to?

1º: If logged in as a certfication authority, you can click the "Your Classics" button in the Navigation Bar, which is available on top of each page.

2º: This will redirect you to the page where a list of classics that have been granted the permission to be certified by you are displayed. Some additional details from each vehicle are also displayed. The button to redirect to each Vehicle Card is also displayed.

Add a New Certification: [Certification authorities exclusive]

As a certification authority, how can I register a new certification?

1º: If logged in as a certfication authority, in the " Certifications & Other Documentation" Tab in the Vehicle Card Page, you will have a " Add Certification" button. Click it.

2º: By clicking the button, you will automatically register the specific classic a new certification.
